Understanding the Problem

What is Penetrating Damp?

Penetrating damp is water entering a building horizontally — or sometimes from above — through defects in the external structure. Unlike rising damp, it is not ground moisture travelling upward; it is rainwater or moisture from outside finding a path through the building envelope.

Common causes include cracked or porous render, eroded mortar joints (failed pointing), defective or blocked gutters and downpipes, failed window or door seals, cracked chimney stacks, flat roof defects and poorly fitted external features. In solid-walled properties — very common across older Hampshire housing stock — the wall itself can become saturated in sustained wet weather.

Penetrating damp patches often appear after heavy rainfall and may dry out in dry spells, which can mask the true extent of the problem. Left untreated, it leads to internal staining, mould growth, plaster damage and — in severe cases — timber rot.

Warning Signs

  • Damp patches that appear or worsen after rainfall
  • Staining on walls or ceilings adjacent to external features
  • Damp concentrated around chimney breasts, window reveals or party walls
  • Mould appearing on internal walls facing outside
  • Visible cracks in render, failed pointing or gaps around window frames
  • Damp patches that dry out in summer but return each winter
Our Approach

How We Diagnose & Treat Penetrating Damp

1

External & Internal Inspection

We inspect the full building exterior — render, pointing, gutters, downpipes, window seals, roof edges and chimney stacks — alongside internal moisture readings to map the path of water entry.

2

Identify & Repair Entry Points

We repair the defects that are allowing water in. This may include repointing, rendering, resealing window frames, repairing or replacing gutters and downpipes, or waterproofing chimney stacks.

3

Waterproofing Treatment

Where masonry is porous, we apply a penetrating waterproof masonry cream or silane/siloxane treatment that soaks deep into the wall and repels water without changing the appearance of the external surface.

4

Internal Remediation

Once the entry point is sealed, we address any internal damage — staining, blown plaster or mould — so your property is left fully restored.

Why Choose Us

Why Choose Damp Solve?

Penetrating damp is only fixed when the entry point is found and sealed. Treating the internal symptoms without addressing the cause — which is unfortunately common — just means the problem returns the following winter.

We always start externally: finding where the water is actually getting in. Only once that's resolved do we address the internal damage. This approach takes longer than applying a tanking coat and painting over it, but it produces results that last.
