Dry Rot Treatment & Eradication in Hampshire

Dry rot is the most serious form of timber decay in UK buildings. It spreads fast, travels through masonry, and if left untreated will cause structural damage. Damp Solve provides specialist survey, eradication and reinstatement — fully stopping dry rot in its tracks.

Understanding the Problem

What is Dry Rot?

Dry rot is caused by the fungus Serpula lacrymans — the most destructive timber decay fungus found in buildings. Despite the name, it requires moisture to get started (typically 20%+ timber moisture content), but once established it can generate its own moisture and spread into apparently dry areas, travelling through masonry, plaster and brickwork to find new timber to attack.

It breaks down the cellulose in timber, leaving it weakened, brittle and crumbling with the distinctive brown cuboid (cuboidal) cracking pattern. A fruiting body — a rust-red, fleshy bracket — produces millions of spores that can spread the outbreak to other areas of the building.

Because dry rot can travel through masonry, tackling only the visible affected timber is not sufficient. The entire extent of the outbreak must be established, the moisture source eliminated, all infected material removed, and the surrounding masonry treated with fungicide. This requires specialist knowledge — and it is not a job for cutting corners.

Warning Signs

  • Distinctive musty, mushroom-like smell
  • Brown, brittle timber with cuboid cracking pattern
  • White or grey cotton-wool-like mycelium (fungal strands)
  • Rust-red spore dust around or beneath timber
  • Fleshy, rust-red fruiting body (bracket) on walls or timber
  • Timber that crumbles under gentle pressure
Our Approach

How We Eradicate Dry Rot

1

Specialist Survey & Extent Mapping

We identify the full extent of the outbreak — not just what is visible. Dry rot travels through masonry, so the real extent is often much larger than the visible damage suggests. We probe, test and map the outbreak before any work begins.

2

Eliminate the Moisture Source

Dry rot cannot survive without moisture. We identify and fix the source — whether that's a leaking pipe, failed DPC, roof defect or rising damp — before any timber work takes place. Without this step, dry rot will always return.

3

Remove All Infected Material

All affected timber is cut back to at least 300–500mm beyond the last visible sign of infection. Infected plaster and masonry are stripped back and the area is thoroughly cleaned to remove spores.

4

Masonry Treatment & Reinstatement

Exposed masonry is treated with a specialist fungicidal preservative. New timber is pre-treated before installation. We reinstate plasterwork and leave the area ready for decoration.
