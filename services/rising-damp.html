Understanding the Problem

What is Rising Damp?

Rising damp occurs when ground moisture travels upward through masonry walls by capillary action — the same process that draws water up a sponge. It affects walls at low level, typically up to around one metre from floor level, though in severe cases it can rise higher.

All masonry is slightly porous. Buildings constructed before the 1920s usually have no damp-proof course at all. Older damp-proof courses made from slate, lead or bitumen can deteriorate over time and fail. When there is no functioning barrier, moisture from the ground — carrying dissolved salts — wicks continuously upward into the wall.

Rising damp is often confused with condensation or penetrating damp. This matters because the wrong diagnosis leads to the wrong treatment. At Damp Solve, we always survey first and recommend only what the problem actually requires.

Warning Signs

  • Tide marks or horizontal staining on lower walls
  • White salt deposits (efflorescence) on wall surfaces
  • Flaking, bubbling or blown plaster near the floor
  • Peeling wallpaper at low level
  • Rotting skirting boards or floor timbers
  • Musty, earthy smell at low level — especially in older homes
Our Approach

How We Diagnose & Treat Rising Damp

1

Free Survey & Moisture Assessment

We visit the property and take moisture meter readings throughout the affected area. We check for any other causes — such as bridging, blocked air bricks or high external ground levels — before confirming the diagnosis.

2

Chemical DPC Injection

We inject a silicone-based damp-proof cream into a continuous course of mortar at the appropriate height. The cream diffuses through the mortar, bonding to form a water-repellent barrier that stops moisture rising further.

3

Hack Off & Re-plaster

Contaminated plaster must be removed — it holds hygroscopic salts that will keep drawing moisture even after the DPC is installed. We re-plaster with a specialist salt-resistant waterproof render that prevents re-contamination.

4

Timber Check & Reinstatement

We check skirting boards and any adjacent floor timbers for damp-related damage and advise on or carry out any necessary replacement before finishing.
