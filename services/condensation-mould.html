Understanding the Problem

What is Condensation Damp?

Condensation occurs when warm, moisture-laden air meets a cold surface — a wall, window or ceiling — and the water vapour it carries turns to liquid. Every household produces moisture through breathing, cooking, bathing and drying clothes. In a well-ventilated home, that moisture escapes. In a poorly ventilated one, it has nowhere to go.

Modern draught-proofing and double glazing, while energy-efficient, can make condensation worse by trapping moist air inside. Older properties with no through-ventilation, single-skin walls or north-facing rooms are particularly susceptible.

The black mould (Cladosporium and Aspergillus species) that grows on condensation-affected surfaces is not just unsightly — it produces spores that affect air quality and can trigger respiratory problems, particularly in children, the elderly and those with asthma. Treating only the visible mould without addressing the ventilation problem means it will always return.

Warning Signs

  • Black or green mould spots, especially in corners and behind furniture
  • Streaming or persistently wet windows
  • Musty smell — particularly in bedrooms and bathrooms
  • Mould growing on clothing, shoes or stored items
  • Peeling wallpaper or bubbling paint on internal walls
  • Worse in winter; may partially clear in warm, dry weather
Our Approach

How We Diagnose & Treat Condensation

1

Humidity & Ventilation Assessment

We measure relative humidity throughout the property, check existing ventilation (extractor fans, air bricks, trickle vents) and identify the rooms and surfaces most affected. We confirm the problem is condensation and not penetrating or rising damp.

2

Mould Treatment

We treat all affected surfaces with a specialist fungicidal biocide that kills existing mould spores and inhibits regrowth. This step is carried out safely and thoroughly — not just wiped over with a surface spray.

3

Ventilation Solution

We recommend and install the appropriate ventilation solution for your property. This may be a Positive Input Ventilation (PIV) unit, improved bathroom and kitchen extraction, or a whole-house mechanical ventilation system depending on the severity.

4

Advice & Aftercare

We explain clearly what changes in lifestyle habits (drying clothes, cooking, heating patterns) will help maintain low humidity, and we give you a written summary of what was found and what was done.
