Understanding the Problem

What is Wet Rot?

Wet rot is the decay of timber caused by fungal attack in conditions of sustained high moisture — typically where timber moisture content exceeds 50%. Several species cause wet rot, the most common being Coniophora puteana (cellar fungus), though Fibroporia vaillantii and others are also frequently found.

Unlike dry rot, wet rot stays where the moisture is — it does not travel through masonry. This makes it less structurally threatening than dry rot in most cases, but it still causes significant damage to windows, door frames, floor joists, skirting boards and any other timbers exposed to sustained moisture.

Common locations include window frames and sills (where paint fails and water penetrates), ground-floor joists in poorly ventilated subfloor voids, lintels above leaking windows, and any timber adjacent to a persistent leak or rising damp. Left untreated, wet rot progressively weakens structural timber and creates ideal conditions for woodworm infestation.

Warning Signs

  • Soft, spongy or crumbling timber that feels weak under pressure
  • Darkening or discolouration of timber (brown or black)
  • Paint bubbling, blistering or peeling from window frames or sills
  • Cracking along the timber grain (not cuboid like dry rot)
  • Musty smell in subfloor void, under stairs or in basement
  • Springy or uneven floorboards (sign of joist decay below)
Our Approach

How We Treat Wet Rot

1

Survey & Moisture Source Identification

We use a calibrated moisture meter to map the full extent of the wet rot and trace it back to its moisture source — whether that's a leaking window, plumbing issue, failed DPC or blocked gulley. Fixing the source comes before any timber work.

2

Eliminate the Moisture Source

The moisture source is repaired. Without this, new timber installed in the same location will decay again within a few years — wet rot cannot be treated permanently while the moisture remains.

3

Timber Treatment or Replacement

Mildly affected timber can be treated with a penetrating preservative consolidant that hardens and stabilises the wood. Severely affected structural timber is cut out and replaced with pre-treated timber of the same specification.

4

Reinstatement & Decoration Prep

We make good the surrounding areas, seal any exposed end grain, and prepare the surface ready for your decorator — leaving the area clean, dry and treated against future attack.

Why Choose Us

Why Choose Damp Solve for Wet Rot?

The most common mistake with wet rot treatment is replacing the timber without fixing the moisture source. The new timber lasts a few years, then decays again — and the homeowner faces the same bill again.

We always fix the cause first. Our approach is to find why the timber got wet, stop it, then treat or replace the timber. We also check surrounding structural timbers to make sure no further decay has been missed.
