What You Get

What is a Damp Survey?

A damp survey is a detailed inspection of a property to determine whether moisture problems are present, what is causing them and what needs to be done to resolve them properly. It is not just a quick look around with a moisture meter. A proper survey considers the building as a whole.

At Damp Solve, we inspect the affected areas, check the likely sources of moisture, assess ventilation and look for associated timber defects such as wet rot, dry rot or woodworm where relevant. We then set out our findings clearly, along with practical recommendations.

Our surveys are useful for homeowners who want answers, landlords and letting agents who need reliable advice, and prospective buyers who want to understand the true condition of a property before committing. If damp is the problem, we can identify it and explain the right solution.

When to Book a Survey

  • You have visible damp patches, staining or damaged plaster
  • You are buying a property and need a clearer picture than a standard homebuyer report
  • You have mould or condensation but are unsure of the cause
  • You need written findings for a landlord, letting agent, solicitor or buyer
  • You want to avoid unnecessary treatment and get an honest diagnosis first
  • You suspect hidden timber problems such as wet rot, dry rot or woodworm
Our Process

How Our Damp Surveys Work

1

Initial Discussion

We begin by understanding what you have noticed, how long the issue has been present and what kind of property we are dealing with. This helps us focus on the most likely causes before we arrive.

2

On-Site Inspection

We inspect the internal and relevant external areas, take moisture readings and check features such as gutters, ventilation, skirting, plaster condition and surrounding timbers. We are looking for the cause, not just the symptoms.

3

Diagnosis & Recommendations

Once we have the evidence, we explain what type of damp or timber problem is present and what action is recommended. If the issue is minor or does not need specialist treatment, we say so clearly.

4

Written Report

Where required, we provide a written report setting out our findings and recommendations in straightforward language. This is especially helpful for buyers, landlords and letting agents who need a record they can refer back to.
